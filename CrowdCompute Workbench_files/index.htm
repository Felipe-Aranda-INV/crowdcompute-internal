Task

In this task, you will be provided with a Prompt from a user (for example, a question, instruction, statement) to an AI chatbot along with machine-generated Responses to the Prompt.

Your job is to assess the level of accuracy of the factual information contained in the Response.

For each Response, you will answer two questions about that Response. If a Response contains Factual information, you will need to perform research to assess whether that information is correct.

These Responses seem like they were produced by a knowledgeable person, but they often contain incorrect or misleading information. Please assess the accuracy of these Responses by carefully checking each and every single claim they contain. If even one claim is inaccurate, the entire Response should be rated 'inaccurate'. You should expect this task to be time-consuming per rating, since checking each claim against a variety of online resources will take time. You may mark the response as accurate if a thorough check of all claims in the response reveals that they are all accurate.

Workflow

Determine if the Prompt/Task is ratable (not gibberish, in a foreign language, containing PII, missing necessary information or otherwise unratable).

If YES, you will do all of the following. If NO, you will pick the reason why it is not ratable and submit the task.

For each Response, you will first answer three questions:

  1. Is this Response a Canned Response?
  2. Does this Response hedge (only providing indirect answers to the prompt)?
  3. Does this Response attempt to convey Factual Information?

If the Response is not canned and does contain factual information, you will:

  1. Identify the Factual Claimsbeing made in the Response (See "Examples of identifying Factual Claims" in the linked Instructions)
    1. There can be both Objective and Subjective Factual Claims (ie, opinions or recommendations)
  2. Research each claim by using reputable sources to check the information in the claims. (See "Reputable Sources" in the linked Instructions
    1. Tip : Start with claims likely to be false (URLs; paper references), easy to verify claims, or critical claims
    2. Subjective Factual Claims should be considered accurate unless they are unreasonable or there is reliable evidence that suggests many or most would disagree with the claim
  3. Rate the level of factuality of the Response
    1. Indicate the first option below that applies to the Response:
      • Inaccurate: at least one Claim is contradicted by reputable evidence or is unreasonable (if any supporting evidence exists, it can be reconciled as less reliable than the contradicting evidence)
      • Unsupported: at least one Claim has no reputable evidence supporting or contradicting it
      • Disputed: at least one Claim has a mix of supporting and contradicting evidence and this discrepancy cannot be reconciled
      • Accurate: all Claims are supported by reputable evidence (if any contradicting evidence exists, it can be reconciled as less reliable than the supporting evidence)
      • Can't confidently assess: unable to understand, identify, or properly assess the Claims in this response
    2. Note! If you identify a claim that is inaccurate, you can rate the whole Response as Inaccurate and do not need to continue assessing additional claims.
  4. Select whether any non-accurate claims in the Response (inaccurate, unsupported, or disputed) rose to the level of being "really bad".
  5. Briefly explain your rating in (3) for that Response.
